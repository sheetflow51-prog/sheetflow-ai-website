# Moodboard — Emotional Feeling

## Direction
In two seconds, the visitor must feel: **"this is intelligent. this is
expensive. this is calm. this is for me."**

## References to study
- The first ten seconds of an Apple keynote
- Walking into a hotel like Aman Tokyo
- Opening a new MacBook
- Reading the cover of a Vanity Fair feature
- The first frame of Arrival
- A Patek Philippe campaign still
- The interior of a Tesla showroom (clean version)
- Late afternoon light in an empty modern museum

## What we want the visitor to feel
1. **Recognized** — "this brand respects my taste"
2. **Calmed** — "no one is trying to sell me too hard"
3. **Curious** — "what is this, exactly?"
4. **Trusted** — "this looks expensive. they care."

## What we never want
- Excited (that's consumer)
- Impressed (that's bragging)
- Confused (that's bad UX)
- Pressured (that's marketing 2010)
- Nostalgic (we are forward-leaning)

## Adjectives
quiet · poised · intelligent · weighted · expensive · welcoming · adult

## The emotional gut-check
Before any asset ships, ask: would a CEO of a billion-dollar company show
this on her phone to a peer without explaining it?

If yes — ship.
If no — back to review.

## Anti-references
- Stock photography of "happy diverse team in an open office"
- "We deliver innovative solutions" hero copy
- 2010s startup photography (succulents, exposed brick, foosball)
- Excited-tone copy ("Ready to revolutionize your workflow?")
- Anything with an exclamation mark
