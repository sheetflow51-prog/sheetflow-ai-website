# Moodboard — Cinematic Framing

## Direction
The eye is guided, not commanded. Composition does the work; nothing screams
for attention.

## References to study
- Roger Deakins compositions (1917, Skyfall, Blade Runner 2049)
- Bradford Young (Arrival opening)
- Greig Fraser (Dune 2021 wide shots)
- Wes Anderson symmetry — for product/portrait reference, not for cinematic
- Vermeer paintings — single window light, off-center subject
- Hopper paintings — emptiness as subject
- Annie Leibovitz Vanity Fair covers

## Adjectives
guided · weighted · negative-space-rich · golden-ratio · still

## Anti-references
- Center-framed everything
- "Action" framing with motion blur
- Dutch angles (drama for drama's sake)
- Symmetry just because (when it isn't earned)
- Foreground noise (out-of-focus leaves, etc.)

## Framing rules
1. **70% rule**: 70% of every cinematic frame is negative space.
2. **Off-center subjects**: thirds or golden ratio. Never dead center
   except for portraits and posters.
3. **One eye anchor per frame**: where the contrast lives.
4. **Headroom on portraits**: 10% of frame, never less.
5. **Horizon never centered** unless framing demands symmetry.
6. **Foreground occlusion**: deliberate or none. Never accidental.
