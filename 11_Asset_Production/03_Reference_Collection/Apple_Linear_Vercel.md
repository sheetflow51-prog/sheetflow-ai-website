# Apple / Linear / Vercel — The Holy Trinity of Restraint

Three companies have already solved most of the problems we're solving.
We don't copy them. We absorb the **principles** behind their decisions.

---

## Apple

### What to learn
- **Hierarchy through size, not color.** A massive number sells more than a banner.
- **Negative space is the message.** Apple often dedicates 60%+ of a frame to nothing.
- **Black is sacred.** True black, not gray-pretending-to-be-black.
- **One subject per frame.** Two compete; one commands.
- **Type as object.** Headlines have weight, position, and breathing room like a product photo.

### What to avoid
- Imitating Apple gradients literally (it dates instantly).
- Overusing the "floating product" composition.
- Trying to match their photography budget — match their **restraint**, not their gear.

### What fits SheetFlow
- The keynote-still mindset on hero sections.
- Hero copy at display sizes (clamp 56–96px equivalent).
- Product imagery that reads at thumbnail and at billboard.

---

## Linear

### What to learn
- **Geometric calm.** Every angle has a reason. No decorative flourishes.
- **Monochrome with one accent.** The whole identity runs on grayscale + a single hue.
- **Density without clutter.** They show a lot; nothing feels crowded.
- **Motion as structure.** Movement reinforces hierarchy, never decorates.
- **Tone of voice = visuals.** Their copy is calm. Their visuals match it.

### What to avoid
- Mistaking minimalism for emptiness. Linear is dense — just controlled.
- Copying their purple. Find your own accent.
- Using their grid system without adapting it to your content.

### What fits SheetFlow
- Single-accent palette philosophy (we lean cool sapphire + warm amber rim).
- Product screenshots styled like editorial frames.
- Documentation that looks like a product, not a manual.

---

## Vercel

### What to learn
- **Gradient as atmosphere.** Their gradients aren't backgrounds — they're light.
- **Dev-product confidence.** Visuals that respect the user's intelligence.
- **Dark mode as default.** And it actually works because of light, not contrast.
- **Marketing that doesn't apologize.** Big claims, clean delivery.
- **Open-source aesthetic.** Sharable, screenshot-friendly, repository-worthy.

### What to avoid
- The "every gradient" pitfall. They earn each one. We must too.
- Triangle motifs (now overused industry-wide).
- Dev-bro aesthetic. Vercel never falls into it; lesser brands do.

### What fits SheetFlow
- The atmospheric-gradient hero treatment.
- Product UI in marketing presented honestly (no fake dashboards).
- Confident headline scale + restrained color.

---

## The Trinity Test

Before any composition ships, answer:

- Could this sit on **apple.com** and not look out of place? (luxury)
- Could this sit on **linear.app** and not look loud? (calm)
- Could this sit on **vercel.com** and not look generic? (atmosphere)

If yes to all three, it might be SheetFlow.
