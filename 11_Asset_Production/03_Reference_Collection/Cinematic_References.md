# Cinematic References

Films and DPs to study. We are making a brand that should fit between
these frames.

---

## Anchor films

| Film                     | DP              | Why it's here                                            |
|--------------------------|-----------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Blade Runner 2049 (2017) | Roger Deakins   | Atmosphere as identity. Negative space. Quiet color.     |
| Arrival (2016)           | Bradford Young  | Stillness. Restraint. Light as emotion.                  |
| Dune (2021)              | Greig Fraser    | Scale through emptiness. Earth tones with depth.         |
| The Social Network (2010)| Jeff Cronenweth | Locked frames, controlled tension, screen-light realism. |
| Foundation (Apple TV+)   | Various         | Holographic UI without RGB cliché.                       |
| Severance (2022)         | Jessica Lee Gagné | Clinical depth. Symmetry that unsettles.               |
| Ex Machina (2014)        | Rob Hardy       | Architecture as character. Glass, concrete, calm.        |
| Her (2013)               | Hoyte van Hoytema | Warm AI. Soft tech. Humanity in machines.              |

---

## What to learn

- **Light is a verb.** Deakins doesn't decorate, he sculpts.
- **Negative space is design.** Fraser uses 70% of the frame for nothing.
- **Color is restraint.** Pick three values and live there.
- **The camera doesn't perform.** Locked, breathing, patient.
- **Atmosphere is haze + light, not effects.**

## What to avoid

- Lens flares as personality
- "Cinematic" LUTs that bleach contrast
- Orange/teal as a default
- Camera moves that announce themselves
- Slow motion when stillness would be stronger

## What fits SheetFlow

- Deakins-grade restraint
- Fraser-grade emptiness
- Hoytema-grade warmth on humans
- Cronenweth-grade screen realism for product shots

## Cinematic observations to absorb

- The most expensive frames are the simplest.
- Skin tone is sacred. If skin looks wrong, nothing else matters.
- Shadow side of the face must remain dark. Don't fill it out of fear.
- A single hard light beats six soft ones.
- The eye goes where contrast lives. Control contrast, control attention.
