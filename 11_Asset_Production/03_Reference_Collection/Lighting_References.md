# Lighting References

Light is the only thing that makes an image expensive. Color is the cheap fix.

---

## Studio setups worth stealing

### Apple keynote product reveal
- Top-down soft key, slightly cool (5600–6000K)
- Black flag below to keep shadow side genuinely black
- Single hard rim, 90–120° behind, sculpts silhouette
- No bounce. Apple is brave with shadow.

### Linear marketing stills
- Diffused window light, single source
- 3:1 key-to-fill ratio (almost no fill)
- Cool ambient, warm spec highlights only
- Backgrounds dark gray, never pure black

### Vercel hero compositions
- Gradient as light, not as color
- Deep blacks, warm whites, no mids
- Glow from inside the subject, not from behind

## Natural light to absorb

| Time of day      | What it teaches                                          |
|------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Pre-dawn         | Cool depth without coldness. Mood without sadness.       |
| Magic hour       | Skin tones at their best. Use sparingly — it's a cliché. |
| Overcast noon    | Even, honest, unromantic. Great for product clarity.     |
| Window with sheer| The most flattering light ever invented. Free.           |
| Single bulb dusk | Intimacy. Founder/portrait reference.                    |

## What to avoid

- Three-point lighting on autopilot. It looks like everyone else's stock.
- Filling shadows because you're nervous. Trust the dark.
- Hard color gels (RGB stage lighting). Cyberpunk poison.
- Bloom as substitute for real volumetric light.
- Ring lights on humans. They flatten.

## What fits SheetFlow

- Cool key, warm rim — the SheetFlow "two-temperature" rule.
- Deep shadows, never milky.
- Volumetric haze 4–6% in 3D scenes — the silent atmosphere lever.
- One dominant source per frame. Period.

## Cinematic observations

- The negative fill is more important than the key. It's what makes the
  image **read as 3D**.
- A 1° change in key angle changes the whole emotion. Move slowly.
- If the highlight on a sphere isn't a clean shape, your light isn't shaped.
- Color temperature contrast is more powerful than hue contrast.
- Natural light has falloff. Fake lights without falloff look fake.
