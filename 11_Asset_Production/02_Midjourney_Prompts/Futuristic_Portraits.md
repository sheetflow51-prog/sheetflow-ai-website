## Hard rules for portraits

1. **Skin must remain warm.** If it goes blue or green, reject.
2. **No smiling.** Composure > performance. Magazine, not stock.
3. **Shadow side dark.** Don't fill the face. Trust the dark side.
4. **One subject per portrait** unless explicitly framed as a pair.
5. **Diverse representation by default.** Generate variations across
   gender, age, ethnicity unless brief specifies an individual.
6. **Real-feeling clothing.** Charcoal merino, white tee, navy blazer.
   Never bright colors. Never logos.
7. **No props that perform.** Coffee, laptops in hero focus, etc.
8. **Hands matter.** Midjourney often misses hands — generate variations,
   pick the cleanest, retouch in Photoshop if needed.

## Replacement for AI portraits

For real founders/customers, do real photography instead. These prompts are
for:
- Concept stills before a real shoot
- Customer placeholder imagery (clearly marked as illustrative)
- Mood references for actual photographers
