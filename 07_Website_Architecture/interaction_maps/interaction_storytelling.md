## 03. Micro-Interaction Stories

### The Particle Field Story
*Narrative*: The visitor arrives in a living world. The world is aware of them.
The particles drifting in the background aren't decoration — they're the data ecosystem.
As the mouse moves, the nearest particles respond — not dramatically, but perceptibly.
The message: *you are already in relationship with this system.*

### The Feature Card Story
*Narrative*: The visitor is an explorer. The cards reward curiosity.
Cards that look sealed on arrival. On hover, they open — reveal their depth.
Hidden content slides up; the card lifts; the border illuminates.
The message: *there is always more than what is immediately visible.*

### The Command Palette Story
*Narrative*: Language is power here.
The command palette demo cycles through natural language queries.
Each one is increasingly impressive — from simple to complex.
The last query should be something that takes hours manually — done in seconds.
The message: *if you can say it, SheetFlow AI can do it.*

### The Live Counter Story
*Narrative*: This is happening right now, as you read this.
Counters that count up when they enter the viewport.
The number was already this high — the counting is just revealing it.
"1,247,832 cells monitored right now" — the word *now* carries enormous weight.
The message: *you are watching a live system. This is already running.*

## 05. The Delight Layer

Beyond functional interactions, SheetFlow AI has a delight layer —
small moments of unexpected quality that accumulate into a premium perception.

### Delight Moments

**The Invisible Grid**
When hovering the feature bento grid, a subtle grid lines appear briefly — like a blueprint revealing itself. Visible for 200ms, then fades. Makes the grid feel engineered.

**The Echo Headline**
The hero headline appears in very large, very faint text behind the final CTA section.
Like an echo — the opening statement resonating at the close.

**The Smart Loading**
If the visitor returns to the page a second time (or the same session), the boot loading sequence is shorter — like a system that remembers the visitor.

**The Responsive Logo**
The logo in the nav very subtly pulses once when the page first loads.
Almost imperceptible — but those who notice feel like they caught something.

**The Data Tooltip**
Hovering over the product UI screenshots shows a miniature tooltip with fake-but-plausible data. "Revenue: $284,102 — up 12% this week". Makes the screenshots feel alive.

## 06. Interaction Pacing Philosophy

### The Goldilocks Zone
Interactions that respond too quickly feel glitchy.
Interactions that respond too slowly feel broken.
The premium zone: fast enough to feel instant, slow enough to feel crafted.

```
Too fast (feels broken):   transition: 50ms or less
Too slow (feels heavy):    transition: 500ms+ for UI states
The premium zone:          150ms–300ms for most UI transitions
                           400ms–700ms for content reveals
```

### The Energy Principle
Each interaction should give back at least as much energy as the user put in.
A light hover should return a light response.
A full click should return a full response.
The system should never feel unresponsive or overwhelming.
