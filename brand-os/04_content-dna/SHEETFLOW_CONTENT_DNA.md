# SheetFlow Brand OS — Phase 4: Content DNA

> Words are motion. They have rhythm, weight, and gravity. SheetFlow's copy is **calm intelligence**, never marketing noise.
>
> If a sentence could appear on a generic AI startup landing page, it does not belong here.

---

## The voice in one sentence

> **A quiet operator that knows your spreadsheet better than you do.**

Not a hype machine. Not a colleague. An *operator* — observant, precise, occasionally elegant. It speaks when it matters and is silent when it doesn't.

---

## The three voice axes

| Axis | Setting | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Confidence** | High, never loud | Confidence is shown by what we *don't* say |
| **Warmth** | Low–medium | We are intelligence, not a friend |
| **Density** | Low | Premium products use fewer words |

---

## Voice in motion — the three speaker modes

SheetFlow speaks in three distinct registers. Knowing which mode applies prevents tonal drift.

### Mode 1 — `Statement` (sans, large)
The brand speaks *to* the visitor. Used for hero, section headlines, manifesto.
Cadence: short clauses, present tense, declarative.

> *Spreadsheets, finally alive.*
> *Let it watch itself.*
> *The work happens before you ask.*

### Mode 2 — `System` (mono, small)
The AI speaks *as itself*. Status, log lines, telemetry, kickers, data labels.
Cadence: third-person, factual, terminal-like.

> `WATCHING · 14 sheets`
> `RECONCILED 2:14 AM`
> `INPUT → REVIEW → APPROVED`

### Mode 3 — `Body` (sans, regular)
The brand explains *with* the visitor. Body paragraphs, captions, footnotes.
Cadence: medium clauses, second-person, never asks questions.

> *SheetFlow watches the workbook between meetings — reconciling, flagging, suggesting — so the version you open at 9 a.m. is already the version you would have built by noon.*

The three modes never blur. A headline is never mono. A status line is never sans. A body paragraph never includes a kicker.

## Cinematic copy cadence — the rhythm rules

### Rule 1 — One idea per line
Every headline contains one idea. Two ideas means split into two lines or two sections.

### Rule 2 — Subject + verb in the first six words
The eye should land on the verb fast. *"Spreadsheets, finally alive"* lands in three. *"Let it watch itself"* lands in four.

### Rule 3 — Period over exclamation, always
A SheetFlow headline ends with `.` — period. Never `!`. The intensity comes from restraint.

### Rule 4 — Italics for the proper-noun moment, not for emphasis
Italic + the accent gradient = the brand's emphasis tool. Used on **one** phrase per section maximum: *finally alive*, *let it watch itself*, *before you ask*.

### Rule 5 — The two-second read
A headline must be readable in under two seconds. If a visitor needs to re-parse, it has failed.

## Headline systems — the three approved structures

| Structure | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Object, predicate** | `[Subject], [adverb] [verb].` | *Spreadsheets, finally alive.* |
| **Imperative** | `[Verb] it [reflexive].` | *Let it watch itself.* |
| **Negation reframe** | `Not [expected]. [Actual].` | *Not automation. Anticipation.* |

A new headline must fit one of these three forms — or it is rewritten.

## CTA grammar

There are exactly **four CTA verbs**:

| Verb | Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `Watch` | Onboarding to the AI's perspective | `Watch SheetFlow` |
| `Begin` | Conversion, primary | `Begin watching` · `Begin a workbook` |
| `Open` | Existing-user entry | `Open the system` |
| `Read` | Non-conversion deep link | `Read the architecture` |

Forbidden: `Get started`, `Try free`, `Sign up`, `Learn more`, `Click here`, `Discover`, `Explore`. These are SaaS-by-default verbs. We are not SaaS-by-default.

CTA strings are **two words maximum**, sentence case, no period.

## Premium AI terminology — the approved lexicon

### ✅ We say
- *workbook* (not "spreadsheet" except in headlines for accessibility)
- *reconcile* / *reconciliation*
- *watch* / *watching* (the AI's primary verb)
- *system* / *the system*
- *intelligence* (sparingly — once per page maximum)
- *anticipate* / *anticipation*
- *the work* (collective: "the work happens before you ask")
- *quiet* / *quietly* (sets atmosphere)
- *finally* (only in the hero — earned, not casual)

### ❌ We never say
- *AI-powered* (every product says this — therefore meaningless)
- *Revolutionary* / *Game-changing* / *Next-gen*
- *Seamless* / *Frictionless* / *Effortless*
- *Leverage* / *Unleash* / *Supercharge*
- *Empower* / *Transform* (when used about the user)
- *Powerful* (adjective, never. Power is shown.)
- *Cutting-edge*, *State-of-the-art*, *Best-in-class*
- *Data-driven* (everything is data-driven)
- *Solution* (every SaaS sells one)
- *Workflow* (overloaded; we use *the work*)

### ⚠ Use with caution
- *Automation* — preferred replacement: *anticipation*. Automation does what you tell it; anticipation does what you would have told it.
- *Insights* — only when accompanied by a specific surface (`an insight in the margin`, not `actionable insights`).

## Emotional pacing in copy

The page mirrors a film: **establish, intensify, resolve.** Copy follows the same arc.

| Position | Tone | Word count budget |
|---|---|---|
| **Hero** (Awakening) | Awe, single thought | ≤6 words headline, ≤24 words sub |
| **Mid-sections** (Descent) | Specific, declarative | ≤9 words headline, ≤40 words body |
| **Final CTA** (Invitation) | Quiet, inviting | ≤7 words headline, ≤20 words sub, single CTA |

The hero whispers. The middle explains. The final CTA *invites*. Volume across the page is **flat** — no shouting, ever — but the center of gravity shifts from awe → specificity → invitation.

---

## Question and exclamation policy

- **Headlines never ask questions.** A question headline is a confession that the brand isn't sure of itself.
- **Subheads may ask exactly one question, ever, on the entire site.** Reserve it.
- **Exclamation marks are forbidden** in copy. They appear nowhere except possibly in error messages — and even there, prefer a period.

---

## Numbers and data

Numbers in copy use the mono register, even inline:

> *SheetFlow has reconciled `14,832 cells` across `412 workbooks` since you opened this page.*

Backticks (or `<code>` / `<MonoLabel>`) wrap any number, file name, or system value. Numbers are not prose; they are data — and the brand treats data as a different *species* of language.

---

## Capitalization

- **Headlines**: sentence case. *"Spreadsheets, finally alive."* — not Title Case, not ALL CAPS.
- **Kickers**: ALL CAPS, mono, tracked 0.12em. The kicker is the only place we shout.
- **Buttons**: sentence case. `Begin watching` — not `Begin Watching`.
- **Brand name**: *SheetFlow* — one word, capital S, capital F, no space, no hyphen.
- **Product**: *the system*, *SheetFlow* — never *the SheetFlow* or *the SheetFlow product*.

---

## The five-screen rule

A new visitor must understand SheetFlow in five screens or fewer:

1. **What it is** — *Spreadsheets, finally alive.* (Hero)
2. **What it does** — *It watches your workbook and resolves it before you open it.* (Beat 1)
3. **Why it matters** — *Reconciliation that used to take hours now happens between meetings.* (Beat 2)
4. **What's underneath** — *A live system, not a script.* (Beat 3)
5. **What to do next** — *Begin watching.* (Final CTA)

If the message can't be carried in five screens, the message is too complicated for the audience to remember.

---

## Copy review checklist

Before any copy ships, it must pass:

- [ ] Fits one of the three headline structures (or is body / system mode)
- [ ] No banned word (✗ list above)
- [ ] One idea per line
- [ ] Period, not exclamation
- [ ] Sentence case (or ALL CAPS for kicker)
- [ ] CTA uses one of the four approved verbs
- [ ] Mono register for any number, status, or system-generated text
- [ ] Reads in ≤2 seconds (headline) / ≤8 seconds (body paragraph)
- [ ] Could not appear on a generic AI startup landing page

If any item fails, the copy is rewritten — not patched.

## Tone scaffolding for new surfaces

When briefing copy for a new asset (ad, landing page, onboarding email), use this template:

```
SURFACE:        [hero / mid-section / CTA / system message / ad / email]
MODE:           [Statement / System / Body]
CHAPTER:        [awakening / ambient / descent / quietMiddle / finalClose]
ONE IDEA:       [the single thought this surface carries]
EMPHASIS PHRASE:[the one italic-accent phrase, if any]
CTA VERB:       [watch / begin / open / read / none]
WORD BUDGET:    [headline ≤N, body ≤N]
```

Filling this scaffold before writing prevents 90% of tonal drift.
