# SheetFlow AI — Headline System

---

## 01. Headline Philosophy

A headline is not a description. It is an event.

The moment a visitor reads the headline, something shifts.
They either lean forward — or they leave.
There is no neutral reaction to a great headline.

SheetFlow AI headlines operate on three levels simultaneously:
1. **Literal** — what it says
2. **Emotional** — what it makes you feel
3. **Aspirational** — what it implies about your future self

## 04. Headline Formulas That Work

### Formula 1: The Contrast Pair
Line 1: The old world / current state
Line 2: The new world / transformed state

```
"Before: you manage your data.
 After: your data manages itself."

"Before: reports take three days.
 After: they're ready when you wake up."
```

### Formula 2: The Unexpected Specificity
A number or specific detail makes a claim feel real.

```
"Your team spends 340 hours a year on reports that SheetFlow AI generates in 4 minutes."

"3am. Your revenue formula breaks. SheetFlow AI catches it. You find out at 9."
```

### Formula 3: The Category Reframe
Name what you are in a way no one has claimed.

```
"The AI Operating System for data work."
"The intelligence layer beneath your spreadsheets."
"The first tool that doesn't require your spreadsheets to be stupid."
```

### Formula 4: The Unanswered Question
End on a question that creates cognitive pull.

```
"What would your team do with 20 hours back every week?"
"What decisions are you making too late because your data moves too slowly?"
"What would it mean if your data could alert you before the problem becomes a problem?"
```

## 05. Headline Anti-Patterns

### Never Use
- "Revolutionary AI-powered spreadsheet solution" (jargon + generic)
- "Transform your workflow with intelligent automation" (meaningless transformation language)
- "The future of spreadsheets is here" (every SaaS says this)
- "Work smarter, not harder" (exhausted idiom)
- "Take your data to the next level" (vague, tired)

### The Test
Read the headline. Could a competitor say the exact same thing?
If yes — the headline is not ours. It belongs to no one.

A SheetFlow AI headline should be *impossibly specific* to what we do and who we are.
