## 06. Competitor Positioning Analysis

### Stripe
**Their Category**: Payment infrastructure
**Positioning**: Infrastructure for the internet economy
**What They Teach Us**: Name the infrastructure layer, not the feature. "We move money" is less powerful than "we are the economic infrastructure for the internet." SheetFlow AI learns: name the operating system, not the tool.

### Linear
**Their Category**: Issue tracking (re-framed as "software for software teams")
**Positioning**: The tool serious builders use
**What They Teach Us**: Premium SaaS wins by signaling *who* uses it, not what it does. Being the tool that "serious teams" use is a category unto itself. SheetFlow AI learns: signal sophistication in the brand, attract sophisticated operators.

### Notion
**Their Category**: Note-taking → workspace → "connected workspace"
**Positioning**: All your work, one tool
**What They Teach Us**: Category expansion requires bold narrative leaps. Don't stay in the box you started in. SheetFlow AI learns: resist being called "AI for spreadsheets" — that is too small.

### Vercel
**Their Category**: Frontend deployment
**Positioning**: The platform for frontend developers
**What They Teach Us**: Becoming the canonical tool for a specific identity ("frontend developer") creates defensible market position. SheetFlow AI learns: own the "data-driven operator" identity completely.
